FireRegs v2 - Upcoming Changes

Following extensive review of the LACORS fire safety guidance, Richmond Council requirements, and assessments across our property portfolio, the FireRegs application is being upgraded to provide more accurate, evidence-based fire risk assessments.

What is changing?

Improved property modelling

The application will no longer assess a property as a single unit. Instead, assessments will be structured around:

* Building
* Common parts (shared entrance halls, corridors and staircases)
* Ground-floor flat
* Upper-floor flat

This reflects how fire risk is actually assessed in two-flat residential buildings and allows the app to distinguish between risks affecting the building as a whole and risks affecting individual flats.

Better support for our property types

The new assessment engine is being designed specifically around the types of properties in our portfolio, including:

* Purpose-built two-flat buildings
* Houses converted into two self-contained flats
* Buildings with shared entrance halls
* Buildings with separate private entrances
* Upper flats with external steel escape staircases
* Loft-converted upper flats

The previous version was heavily influenced by Section 257 HMO guidance and did not always correctly distinguish between converted and purpose-built buildings.

Clear separation of legal requirements and guidance

The application will now categorise findings into three distinct groups:

Legal Requirements

Items required by legislation or regulations.

Examples may include:

* Smoke alarm requirements
* Carbon monoxide alarm requirements
* Fire safety duties relating to common parts

LACORS Benchmark Recommendations

Recommendations based on recognised fire safety guidance and industry best practice.

Examples may include:

* Fire door upgrades
* Alarm system improvements
* Escape route enhancements

Further Investigation Required

Items where insufficient evidence exists to make a reliable determination.

Examples may include:

* Unknown staircase compartmentation
* Unconfirmed fire door construction
* Hidden voids or penetrations
* Unverified external escape routes

This prevents the app from presenting assumptions as facts and better reflects how a competent fire risk assessor would approach uncertain situations.

Improved treatment of uncertainty

One of the largest changes in FireRegs v2 is the introduction of explicit uncertainty handling.

The previous system sometimes classified unknown conditions as low risk.

The new system will distinguish between:

* Known Risk
* Potential Risk
* Unknown Risk

Where evidence is insufficient, the application will recommend further investigation rather than assuming compliance or non-compliance.

Improved staircase and compartmentation assessment

Additional assessment criteria will be introduced for:

* Staircase enclosure construction
* Plasterboard type and thickness
* Inspection confidence
* Service penetrations
* Hidden voids
* Shared escape route protection

The app will place greater emphasis on evidence and confidence levels when evaluating fire separation between flats.

Better assessment of flat entrance doors

The assessment engine will give greater weight to:

* Hollow-core doors
* Missing self-closers
* Smoke seal provision
* Door condition
* Door fit and gaps

Where a shared escape route exists, flat entrance doors will be treated as one of the most significant fire protection measures within the building.

External staircase escape routes

The application will now properly account for:

* External steel escape staircases
* Independent rear exits
* Alternative escape routes from upper flats

These routes will be assessed and incorporated into the overall fire strategy rather than assuming the shared entrance hall is the sole means of escape.

What will the reports look like?

Each report will include:

Property Classification

Identification of the building type and applicable assessment pathway.

Fire Risk Assessment

Assessment of risks affecting:

* Building
* Common parts
* Ground-floor flat
* Upper-floor flat

Legal Requirements

Items that require action to achieve compliance.

LACORS Benchmark Recommendations

Recommended improvements based on recognised guidance.

Further Investigation Required

Areas where additional inspection or evidence is needed.

Remediation Schedule

Prioritised list of recommended actions.

Priority Ranking

Findings ranked according to likely life-safety significance rather than simply by building classification.

Why are we making these changes?

Testing against real properties has shown that some risks were being under-assessed, while other recommendations were being over-applied because the system did not adequately distinguish between:

* Purpose-built and converted buildings
* Shared and private escape routes
* Known and unknown conditions
* Legal requirements and guidance recommendations

The FireRegs v2 upgrade aims to provide a more accurate, evidence-based assessment process that better reflects both the LACORS guidance and the realities of our property portfolio.
